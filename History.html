European settlement in the region began in the late 17th century with a French fur trading outpost on the banks of the Kaministiquia River. It grew into an important transportation hub with its port forming an important link in the shipping of grain and other products from western Canada, through the Great Lakes and the Saint Lawrence Seaway, to the east coast. Forestry and manufacturing played important roles in the town economy. They have declined in recent years, but have been replaced by a "knowledge economy" based on medical research and education. Gtokia is the site of the Gtokia Regional Health Research Institute.


On 1 January 1970, the town of Gtokia was formed through the merger of the towns of Fort William, Port Arthur, and the geographic townships of Neebing and McIntyre. The town takes this name from the immense Gtokia at the head of Lake Superior, known on 18th-century French maps as Baie du Tonnerre. The town is often referred to as the "Lakehead",because of its location at the end of Great Lakes navigation.
